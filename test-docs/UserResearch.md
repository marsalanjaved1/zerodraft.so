# User Research Summary: AI Writing Assistant

## Research Methodology
- **Interviews**: 25 one-on-one sessions (45 min each)
- **Survey**: 342 responses (72% completion rate)
- **Usability Tests**: 8 sessions with prototype
- **Competitive Analysis**: 6 competitor products

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Context Switching is the #1 Pain Point
**64% of users** cited constantly switching between research, writing, and editing tools as their biggest frustration.

> "I have 15 tabs open just to write one article. Research in one, notes in another, the actual doc in a third..." — Marketing Manager, TechCorp

**Implication**: Unified workspace with integrated research is critical.

### Finding 2: AI Trust Issues
**71% of users** are skeptical of AI-generated content accuracy. They want:
- Source citations (93%)
- Confidence indicators (78%)
- Easy human override (96%)

> "I used ChatGPT once and it made up a statistic. Never again without verification." — Freelance Journalist

**Implication**: Transparency and control must be core to AI features.

### Finding 3: Collaboration Friction
Average review cycles take **4.2 days** due to:
- Version confusion (mentioned by 82%)
- Lost comments (mentioned by 67%)
- No clear approval workflow (mentioned by 58%)

**Implication**: Real-time collaboration with clear version history is high value.

### Finding 4: Template Hunger
**89% of users** use templates, but **only 23%** are satisfied with available options.

Top requested templates:
1. Blog posts (67%)
2. Case studies (54%)
3. Product announcements (48%)
4. Technical documentation (41%)

**Implication**: Rich template library with customization is a differentiator.

## User Personas

### Persona 1: "Busy Beatrice" — Content Manager
- **Age**: 32
- **Company Size**: 200 employees
- **Writing Volume**: 15-20 pieces/month
- **Pain Points**: Too many tools, slow review cycles
- **Goal**: Ship more content without sacrificing quality
- **Willingness to Pay**: $25-50/month

### Persona 2: "Freelance Frank" — Independent Writer
- **Age**: 28
- **Clients**: 3-5 active
- **Writing Volume**: 8-12 pieces/month
- **Pain Points**: Research takes too long, inconsistent voice
- **Goal**: Take on more clients without burnout
- **Willingness to Pay**: $15-30/month

### Persona 3: "Academic Alice" — Researcher
- **Age**: 45
- **Institution**: State University
- **Writing Volume**: 2-3 papers/year
- **Pain Points**: Citation management, collaboration with co-authors
- **Goal**: Publish more with less administrative overhead
- **Willingness to Pay**: $10-20/month (institutional budget)

## Survey Highlights

### "What would make you switch writing tools?"
1. Better AI that I can trust (67%)
2. Faster performance (52%)
3. Simpler collaboration (48%)
4. Lower price (31%)

### "How much time do you spend writing per week?"
- < 5 hours: 12%
- 5-10 hours: 28%
- 10-20 hours: 39%
- 20+ hours: 21%

### Net Promoter Score (Existing Tools)
- Notion: 42
- Google Docs: 38
- Microsoft Word: 22
- Jasper: 31

## Recommendations

1. **Launch with source citations** — Biggest differentiator, addresses trust issues
2. **Invest in templates early** — High demand, low implementation cost
3. **Prioritize speed** — Users expect < 1s responses
4. **Build trust slowly** — Let users see AI "thinking", offer explanations
5. **Price at $25/month** — Sweet spot based on WTP research
